It can't really be said that I'm a very handsome guy. I constantly gained weight because of the computer.

Because of my early curiosity about software and hacking, my grades were pretty bad. When I started 12th grade for the YKS, even though I decided to quit the computer, I started sneaking in some sessions again from time to time. When there were only 3 months left until the exam, one day my chemistry teacher suddenly showed up at our house unannounced. "Enes isn't studying, from now on I'm going to mentor him. He's going to pass this exam; he's a very smart student," he said (he had only realized that I was the one sending SMS bomber and call bomb to his phone during class). Even though I was in the numerical track, I had gotten a 40k ranking in the equal-weight category that year.

When I started university, things had changed a bit. Since I had hated studying my whole life, that 3-month marathon in 12th grade had created a burnout effect on me, and my first year at university passed with me just lying around.

Between the summers of my 1st and 2nd years, I suddenly had an ego explosion and, thinking "I've been in this field since high school anyway," I went straight to the door of a cybersecurity company. Even though the guy said there was no spot for me, I stubbornly begged "bro, please." In the end, the guy kicked me out saying "nothing will come of what you know" (in a kinder version, of course). This incident shattered my ego so badly that I went home that evening and cried, then took out my notebook and prepared an 8-week cybersecurity study plan.

In the 4th week of that 8-week marathon, when I found an RCE in Logo Software's J-Platform product, I gave in to my ego and quit the training. When the 2nd year started, I founded a cybersecurity club at my university and became its president.
